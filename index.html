Background

The name's Jason, a 4th year college student currently studying Electrical and Computer Engineering at Cornell University. Raised in Brooklyn, the city that never sleeps is where I draw much of my inspiration. I believe that in order to truly appreciate well-crafted hardware or elegantly-designed software code, it is always important to see the underlying design. Developing these technological platforms takes motivation, ambition, and hard-work, qualities I exhibit both consistently and comfortably in any environment. Whether I am designing and analyzing electrical systems or volunteering in my community, my passion for electronics grounds me and remains my foundation.

Recent Activities

During the summer this past year, I interned at a company called Insitu. Insitu is a subsidiary of The Boeing Company focused on designing, developing, and supporting unmanned aircraft systems in the aerospace industry. Here is some of the work I did:

  • Developed an acceptance test procedure to verify functionality of engine board for unmanned aircraft systems, including continuity andd resistivity tests, sensor monitoring, voltage measurements, and firmware calibration.
  • Designed filters for engine board circuitry using high-frequency analysis, protected power supplies against kickback voltages through the use of fuses, and performed power analysis on various circuit components.
  • Performed part stress analysis to characterize things such as power dissipation and voltage applications.
  • Became familiar with highly accelerated life testing and drafted a procedure outlining the process for the engine board.

I have also been working on my own pair of portable speakers, hopefully ones that are loud enough to listen to while playing basketball outdoors. I make use of the LM386 low voltage audio power amplifier powered by a 9V battery supply. The speakers used have an impedance of 8Ohms and a power rating of 1W, enough to listen to them comfortably in large rooms. I am incorporating design changes with time, thinking about where certain bypass capacitors should go to remove high frequency oscillations or how to properly protect the speakers by AC coupling the output of the amplifier. I would also like to include Bluetooth capability and work with a microcontroller such as the Arduino to provide indicator LEDs that flash with the beat of a song.
