About Me

I am an open-minded and ambitious individual with a passion for many different things. A big reason why I decided to dedicate my life to electrical and computer engineering was because of the aftermath of Hurricane Sandy. After the storm hit, I realized how dependent both my family and my community were on electricity. Without it, it was difficult to keep food fresh, get around to different places, and communicate with others over the phone. Some of the houses my friends lived in were devastated, and I saw too many families struggle to get by without electricity. I was driven to help as much as I could, and it really pushed me to learn about electricity and why it was so pervasive in society. My parents were supportive in my decision and always inspired me to choose my own path. The fact that electronics worked fascinated me, and studying at Cornell was the perfect opportunity for me to give back to my community.

But I am also more than just my major. I love to do activities outside of engineering and working on my own projects, including playing basketball, playing guitar, singing, and dancing. In high school, I found out I had a passion for hip-hop and breakdancing, and I always made sure I took time to practice my footwork. I try to remain as active as I can and plan my work around my physical activities. It is easier to be mentally alert if I am physically healthy, so I prioritize my health first. I also like making origami paper cranes and taking photographs in my spare time, as I enjoy the balance they provide in my life. Here are some photos I have taken around my neighborhood.

Like most people, I also enjoy listening to music and reading Harry Potter books. Keeping in touch with my friends is also very important, so I always make time to catch up with them during breaks or when I'm available. My family is also very important, and I make it a point to avoid using technology when I am spending time with them. I try to enjoy the moments I can and I really cherish my relationships with other people.
