Volunteer Work

What I love most about the city is the opportunity to give back to my community. I take pride in being more than just an electrical and computer engineer. My passion for electronics is clear, but I also value the power of human connection and being able to help those that are either less fortunate than me or help with events that inspire the Asian-American community. Growing up, my family has always emphasized kindness and willingness to serve others. I have followed my mom's advice all my life. It doesn't matter how smart, how talented, or how hard-working I am. It is more important to prioritize my relationships with other people and to always stay humble.

Red Cross

What I love about the Red Cross is how much influence they have all over the world and how charitable their efforts have been. Through the Red Cross, I have worked with others in different communities and reached out to improve their lives. I have helped organize different walks for my high school such as the breast cancer walk and Light the Night, raised money for different fundraisers such as selling used books to locals, and helped set up events for various communities. I have improved the camraderie among my classmates by making sure the events are exciting and people feel as if they are doing meaningful work. I woke up very early many days to take the subway for hours at a time into different boroughs just to set everything up before other volunteers arrived.

New York Aquarium

At the New York Aquarium, I was a volunteer docent after Hurricane Sandy devastated the Coney Island area, a place dear to my heart. There, I trained for weeks before educating families and their children about marine wildlife, Coney Island's aquatic animals, and the penguin and coral exhibits. I helped with the sea lion aquatheater shows and also inspired the community by working at the touch tank and teaching people to care about the animals. In doing so, I tried helping bring the community together through a love for wildlife and education. Along the way, I gained confidence speaking with others and developed creative ways to convey information to visitors through the use of artifacts. I also took initiative with a group of my close friends and raised over $400 for the New York Aquarium in their rebuilding efforts after Hurricane Sandy. It was known as "Bubbles for Charity", where we set up tied ropes and buckets of soap on the boardwalk. As people walked by, they could make as many large bubbles as they wanted and donate however much money they could to the aquarium. I not only gained valuable time and budget management skills, but I also learned how effective such a small idea could be and how willing the community was to come together in times of need.

Chinatown Youth Initiative

Growing up as an Asian American in New York, I've always felt a sense of belonging in Chinatown. That was why I felt it was so important to give back to the Asian American community and help set up events that would bring us closer together. I've helped set up fairs that families could go to, where their children would enjoy different inflatable obstacles, parents could sit around, relax, and enjoy the atmosphere, and competitive spirits could play different games including life-sized Chinese chess. Because Chinatown is so densely populated, there is a lot of garbage and litter on the ground especially after major holidays such as Chinese New Year. I have taken time out to help clean up the community with others while enjoying the festivities and donating money to the lion dancers that put on performances. I have also attended different open mic sessions hosted by the teen resource center to inspire confidence in others and show that the arts are just as important as STEM fields. I would put on different dances and also support other Asian Americans by staying for their performances. I was exposed to amazing live music, very talented poets, and learned about both the struggles and successes of people growing up as Asian Americans.
